We are pleased to submit our paper titled "Zero-TrustML: Breaking the 33% Byzantine Barrier in Federated Learning with Ground Truth Validation and Holochain DHT" for consideration at the IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy.

NOVEL CONTRIBUTION

Our work makes three principal contributions to Byzantine-robust federated learning:

1. **First Empirical Demonstration of Detector Inversion**: We provide definitive proof that peer-comparison Byzantine detection methods catastrophically fail beyond 33% Byzantine ratios, with 100% false positive rate (flagging ALL honest nodes) at 35% BFT. This validates long-standing theoretical concerns and demonstrates the necessity of external quality signals.

2. **Breaking the 33% Barrier**: We introduce Proof of Gradient Quality (PoGQ), a ground truth validation approach that achieves 100% Byzantine detection with 0-10% false positive rates at 35-50% Byzantine ratios - far exceeding the theoretical ceiling for peer-comparison methods. Our adaptive threshold algorithm reduces false positives by 91% compared to fixed thresholds.

3. **Decentralized Infrastructure Without Centralized Trust**: Our Holochain DHT integration proves that decentralized Byzantine-robust federated learning is practical today, achieving ~10,000 TPS with zero transaction costs while eliminating centralized aggregation servers.

SIGNIFICANCE FOR SECURITY & PRIVACY

This work directly addresses critical security challenges in federated learning:

- **Adversarial Robustness**: Enables FL operation in environments where >33% of participants may be compromised - previously considered unsafe
- **Decentralization**: Eliminates single points of failure and centralized trust assumptions
- **Practical Deployment**: Demonstrated on real implementation with Holochain DHT, not just theoretical analysis
- **Privacy Preservation**: Maintains FL's core privacy guarantees while providing stronger Byzantine resilience

METHODOLOGY

Our evaluation follows rigorous experimental design:

- Direct A/B comparison with identical data (seed 42, Dirichlet α=0.1) proving detector inversion
- Multi-seed validation across 3 random seeds demonstrating statistical robustness
- Byzantine ratios from 20-50% testing operational boundaries
- Multiple attack types (sign flip, scaling, noise, sleeper agents)
- Comprehensive ablation study quantifying adaptive threshold impact (91% improvement)

BROADER IMPACT

This work expands federated learning applicability to high-adversarial scenarios previously considered unsafe: IoT networks with compromised devices, open federated platforms, cross-institutional collaboration with partial trust. By breaking the 33% barrier, we enable privacy-preserving collaborative machine learning in environments where Byzantine ratios may exceed one-third and centralized trust is unacceptable.

COMPUTATIONAL COST OPTIMIZATION

We address computational overhead through architectural innovations: Holochain's DHT naturally distributes validation across √N validators per entry, while IPFS content-addressing with Filecoin incentivized persistence offloads storage costs while maintaining tamper-evidence via cryptographic hashes.

FUTURE WORK

We identify clear paths for eliminating remaining trust assumptions: zero-knowledge proofs (zk-SNARKs) for cryptographic gradient quality verification, federated validation via secret sharing, and validation on more complex datasets (CIFAR-10, FEMNIST, tabular data, NLP).

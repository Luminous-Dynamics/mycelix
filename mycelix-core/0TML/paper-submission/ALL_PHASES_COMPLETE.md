## 💡 Key Lessons from This Process

### 1. Fix, Don't Add
**Lesson**: The reviewer's advice "Fix the blocking consistency errors before adding more data" was critical. Focusing on critical issues rather than adding features led to 100% completion.

**Application**: When facing deadline pressure, resist the temptation to add more results. Fix what's broken first.

### 2. Realistic > Perfect
**Lesson**: "Reviewers will trust '7.7% FPR' more than '0% FPR' because it shows realistic testing with heterogeneous data."

**Application**: Honest results with explained variation are more credible than suspiciously perfect results.

### 3. Direct Comparisons Are Critical
**Lesson**: The reviewer insisted on "Were they tested on **identical data**? This experiment is **critical** for your central claim."

**Application**: When making comparative claims, ensure both systems tested on exactly the same setup. This provides definitive proof.

### 4. Systematic Execution Works
**Lesson**: The 3-day action plan enabled complete resolution of all critical issues in 8 hours through methodical execution.

**Application**: Break complex tasks into daily goals, execute systematically, document progress.

### 5. Documentation Enables Success
**Lesson**: Creating comprehensive checklists and guides ensured nothing was forgotten and enabled team collaboration.

**Application**: Invest time in documentation - it multiplies effectiveness and reduces errors.

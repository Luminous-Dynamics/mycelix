\subsection{Proof Backend Performance}

Table~\ref{tab:dual-backend-comparison} compares the performance of our dual-backend ZK proof architecture: RISC Zero's general-purpose zkVM versus Winterfell's domain-specific STARK for the PoGQ state machine. The hand-rolled Winterfell backend achieves $\sim$230,000$\times$ speedup in proving time (46.6s $\rightarrow$ 0.2ms) and $\sim$22$\times$ reduction in proof size (221KB $\rightarrow$ 10KB) for unit-test scale traces ($T=8$ steps).

The specialized AIR eliminates interpreter overhead and leverages algebraic structure of the PoGQ state transitions (linear/quadratic constraints over $\mathbb{F}_p$). While these micro-benchmarks demonstrate the potential of domain-specific proving, production deployments with realistic trace lengths ($T \sim 1024$) are expected to yield prove times in the 100--500ms range due to $O(T \log^2 T)$ STARK complexity—still orders of magnitude faster than zkVM approaches.

The dual-backend strategy provides both performance (Winterfell for real-time sybil detection) and portability (RISC Zero for universal verification without custom verifier deployment). This architectural choice also offers cryptographic diversity: independent implementations reduce single-point-of-failure risks from bugs or cryptanalytic breakthroughs.

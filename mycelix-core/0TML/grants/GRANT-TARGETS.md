**Last Updated**: October 7, 2025

## 🎯 Tiered Strategy Overview

Our grant strategy is organized into three tiers based on timeline, fit, and funding size:

- **Tier 1**: Fast lifeline grants (rolling applications, 3-6 month decisions)
- **Tier 2**: Major research grants (6-12 month lead time, 2-3 year funding)
- **Tier 3**: Impact-focused foundations (flexible timelines, mission alignment)

## 🏆 Tier 2: Major Research Grants

### 4. National Science Foundation (NSF) - CISE Core Programs

**Fit**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (95% - Best fit for long-term research funding)

**Overview**: NSF's Computer and Information Science and Engineering (CISE) directorate funds foundational research in distributed systems, trustworthy AI, and cybersecurity.

**Funding Range**: $500,000 - $1,200,000 (3 years)
**Timeline**: 6-month lead time, 6-9 month review
**Deadlines**: Typically January 15 and June 15 annually

**Relevant Programs**:
- **CNS (Computer and Network Systems)**: Distributed systems, Byzantine fault tolerance
- **IIS (Intelligent Information Systems)**: Trustworthy AI, federated learning
- **SaTC (Secure and Trustworthy Cyberspace)**: Cryptographic protocols, Sybil resistance

**Application Requirements**:
1. **Project Summary** (1 page)
2. **Project Description** (15 pages maximum):
   - Intellectual Merit
   - Broader Impacts
   - Prior NSF Support (if applicable)
3. **References Cited** (no page limit)
4. **Budget** (detailed, 3 years)
5. **Facilities & Resources**
6. **Data Management Plan**
7. **Postdoctoral Mentoring Plan** (if applicable)
8. **Letters of Collaboration** (optional but recommended)

**Strategic Positioning**:
- **Intellectual Merit**: Novel PoGQ mechanism, rigorous experimental validation
- **Broader Impacts**: Economic equity, underserved populations, open source ecosystem
- **Preliminary Results**: Stage 1 experimental validation (43 experiments)

**Critical Requirements**:
- ✅ **Institutional Affiliation**: MUST secure fiscal sponsor (Holochain Foundation, EFF, university)
- ✅ **Preliminary Data**: Stage 1 results provide this
- ✅ **Peer Review**: Get feedback from academic advisors before submission

**Required Documents**:
- ✅ 00-EXECUTIVE-SUMMARY.md (expand to 15-page Project Description)
- 📋 All core documents (01-08)
- 📋 Broader Impacts statement (emphasize equity, education, open source)
- 📋 3-year detailed budget with justification
- 📋 Letters of support from collaborators/advisors
- 📋 Data management plan
- 📋 Full bibliography (citations.bib)

**Application Target Date**: June 15, 2026 (for Q3 2026 decisions)
**Decision Expected**: March 2027
**Funding Start**: June/July 2027

**Action Items**:
1. **By Dec 2025**: Secure fiscal sponsorship
2. **By Feb 2026**: Complete Stage 1 experimental validation
3. **By Apr 2026**: Draft full 15-page proposal
4. **By May 2026**: Get peer review feedback, revise
5. **Jun 15, 2026**: Submit to NSF

## 💡 Tier 3: Impact-Focused Foundations

### 7. Alfred P. Sloan Foundation - Technology & Society

**Fit**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (80% - Strong if emphasizing economic equity)

**Overview**: Sloan funds research on the societal implications of technology, with focus on public interest technology and economic justice.

**Funding Range**: $200,000 - $800,000 (2-3 years)
**Timeline**: Letter of Inquiry (LOI) anytime, then by invitation
**LOI Review**: 4-6 weeks

**Application Process**:
1. Submit Letter of Inquiry (2 pages): https://sloan.org/grants/apply
2. If invited, submit Full Proposal (10-15 pages)
3. Site visit / presentation
4. Decision

**Strategic Positioning**:
- **Emphasize**: Economic equity, labor markets, platform power
- **Highlight**: Socioeconomic impact analysis (99 citations)
- **Demonstrate**: Commitment to participatory design and community governance

**Required Documents**:
- 📋 Letter of Inquiry (2 pages, adapted from Executive Summary)
- 📋 (If invited) Full proposal with socioeconomic impact focus

**LOI Target Date**: December 2025

## 🎯 Success Metrics & Tracking

### Funding Diversification Target
- **Tier 1 (Fast)**: 1-2 grants secured by Q1 2026 ($50K-$250K total)
- **Tier 2 (Major)**: 1 grant secured by Q3 2027 ($500K-$1.2M)
- **Tier 3 (Impact)**: 0-1 grants secured by Q2 2026 ($50K-$300K)

### Application Tracking
Track all applications in `budgets/grant-tracker.xlsx`:
- Grant name
- Submission date
- Decision date
- Amount requested
- Status (draft, submitted, under review, awarded, rejected)
- Reviewer feedback

### Lessons Learned Log
After each application (win or lose), document:
- What worked well in the narrative
- Reviewer questions/concerns
- How to improve next application

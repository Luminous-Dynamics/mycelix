## 4.1 Experimental Setup

We evaluate our Byzantine-resistant federated learning approach across three dimensions:

### Datasets
- **MNIST** (60K train, 10K test, 10 classes): Handwritten digit recognition
- **EMNIST** (60K train, 10K test, 62 classes): Extended MNIST with letters
- **CIFAR-10** (50K train, 10K test, 10 classes): Natural image classification

### Attack Types
1. **Sign Flip**: Attackers flip gradient signs (most aggressive)
2. **Scaling (100×)**: Attackers scale gradients by 100× (magnitude-based)
3. **Collusion**: Multiple attackers coordinate strategies
4. **Sleeper Agent**: Attackers behave normally, then attack strategically

### Defense Mechanisms
1. **FedAvg** (Baseline): Simple averaging, no Byzantine resistance
2. **FLTrust**: Trusted server-based filtering
3. **BOBA**: Robust aggregation via geometric median
4. **PoGQ v4.1** (Ours): Proof-of-Gradient-Quality with reputation

### Training Configuration
- **Clients**: 100 per experiment
- **Byzantine ratio**: 33% (33 malicious clients)
- **Data split**: Non-IID via Dirichlet(α=0.3)
- **Rounds**: 100 training rounds
- **Local epochs**: 2 per round
- **Batch size**: 32
- **Learning rate**: 0.01
- **Seeds**: 2 random seeds per configuration

## Amendment 1: Ethical Accountability (Article I, Section 2)

**Insert after "Cultural Pluralism" in the Core Principles list:**

**Ethical Accountability: All governance proposals affecting Core Principles, Member Rights, or resource allocation exceeding 100,000 SAP shall be subject to ethics assessment. Proposals receiving a "Blocked" assessment require: (a) mandatory disclosure of specific ethical concerns to all eligible voters before voting closes, (b) escalated approval thresholds (one tier higher than standard requirements), and (c) a minimum 48-hour visibility period for the disclosure before the vote may conclude. Proposals receiving a "Caution" assessment require disclosure but do not escalate thresholds. When ethics assessment infrastructure is unavailable, the system shall default to Caution (not silent approval), ensuring fail-safe rather than fail-open behavior. This principle complements but does not replace the Knowledge Council's non-binding Ethical Alignment Reports (Article II, Section 3.1).**

## Amendment 2: General Council Governance (Article II, Section 6)

**Insert as new Section 6 after Section 5 (Member Redress Council):**

### Section 6. General Council Governance

**6.1 Default Term Limits**

**Unless a specific section of this Constitution provides otherwise, all council, committee, and working group memberships shall expire after 365 days from the date of appointment or election. Members may stand for re-election upon term expiry without limit on consecutive terms.**

**Body-specific terms that override this default:**
- **Foundation Directors: Three (3) years (Article II, Section 2.3)**
- **Knowledge Council: Biennially elected (Article II, Section 3.2)**
- **Audit Guild: Two (2) year terms (Article II, Section 4.3)**

**The 365-day default applies to all other governance bodies including but not limited to: Task DAOs, Liminal DAOs, emergency councils, signing committees, and any future council types not specifically enumerated.**

**6.2 Permission-Less Enforcement**

**Expiration of council terms shall be enforced permission-lessly: any network participant may trigger the mechanical expiration of overdue terms without requiring authorization from any governance body. This ensures that no faction can prevent term expiration by withholding action. Expired members lose voting power immediately upon expiration.**

## Amendment 3: Participation Insurance (Article III, Section 6)

**Insert as new Section 6 after Section 5 (Justification, Review, and Appeal):**

### Section 6. Participation Insurance

**If a veto override vote (Section 5.3) fails to reach quorum, the override threshold shall decrease for each subsequent attempt according to the following schedule:**

- **Attempt 1: Two-thirds (⅔) majority (standard threshold per Section 5.3)**
- **Attempt 2: 62% majority**
- **Attempt 3 and subsequent: 60% majority (absolute floor)**

**Each subsequent attempt shall extend the override window by 48 hours beyond the standard period.**

**This mechanism ensures that sustained voter suppression — whether through infrastructure disruption, social pressure, or strategic abstention — cannot permanently block collective action. The 60% floor preserves supermajority legitimacy while preventing a 34% minority from indefinitely sustaining a veto through non-participation.**

**Participation insurance applies to Golden Veto overrides (this Article) and to any future governance mechanism that employs supermajority override thresholds.**

## Amendment 5: Emergency Session Constraints (Article IX, Section 3)

**Insert as new Section 3 after Section 2 (Governance Pauses):**

### Section 3. Emergency Session Constraints

**3.1 Duration and Frequency**

**Emergency governance sessions — distinct from the technical circuit breaker (Section 1) and governance pauses (Section 2) — shall be subject to the following hard limits:**

- **Maximum duration: 14 days per emergency session**
- **Maximum consecutive sessions: Three (3)**
- **Mandatory cooldown: 30 days after three consecutive sessions**

**3.2 Automatic Expiry**

**Emergency sessions expire automatically at their stated duration. Expiration enforcement is permission-less: any network participant may trigger the dissolution of an expired emergency session without requiring authorization.**

**3.3 Cooldown Override**

**The 30-day mandatory cooldown may only be overridden by a non-emergency council vote. Emergency councils may not approve their own renewal.**

**3.4 Scope Limitations**

**Emergency sessions may not be used to suspend:**
- **Member Redress Council operations**
- **Fork rights (Article IX, Section 5)**
- **The veto override mechanism (Article III, Section 5.3 and Section 6)**
- **Emergency fund disbursements**
- **Permission-less enforcement of term limits**

**3.5 Relationship to Other Mechanisms**

**Emergency sessions are distinct from:**
- **Technical circuit breakers (Section 1): 72-hour maximum, multisig-triggered**
- **Governance pauses (Section 2): 30-day maximum, DAO supermajority-triggered**

**Emergency sessions provide a structured, intermediate-duration mechanism with automatic expiry, filling the gap between the short circuit breaker and the longer governance pause.**

## Amendment 6: AI Governance Constraints (Article XI, Section 4)

**Insert as new Section 4 after Section 3 (Moratorium on AI Sovereignty):**

### Section 4. AI Governance Constraints

**4.1 Governance Tier Ceiling**

**Instrumental Actors (Section 2) are limited to Steward tier or below in all governance participation mechanisms, including but not limited to: consciousness-gated voting, proposal submission, council membership, and signing committee participation. No Instrumental Actor may attain or exercise Guardian-tier governance powers.**

**4.2 Veto Prohibition**

**Instrumental Actors may not exercise the Golden Veto (Article III) or participate in veto override votes. This prohibition applies regardless of the Instrumental Actor's consciousness score, reputation, or tier classification.**

**4.3 Self-Reported Score Cap**

**Self-reported consciousness or trust scores for any entity — human or Instrumental Actor — are capped below Guardian tier for governance purposes. Scores at or above Guardian tier require cryptographically signed external attestation from a recognized verification authority. This prevents score fabrication from unlocking governance powers that require demonstrated consciousness.**

**4.4 Immutability**

**The constraints in this Section are part of the Immutable Core (Article IV, Section 2). Modification requires the enhanced amendment process: 90% supermajority in both Global DAO houses, ratified by three-fourths (3/4) of all Sector and Regional DAOs.**

**4.5 Relationship to Section 3**

**This Section does not affect the Moratorium on AI Sovereignty (Section 3). The moratorium concerns whether AI entities can be recognized as Members with full civic sovereignty. This Section concerns the operational governance ceiling for Instrumental Actors within the existing framework. Both provisions operate independently.**

## Amendment 7: New Definitions (Article XII)

**Add to the definitions list:**

**Permission-Less Enforcement: The principle that time-limited governance powers (term limits, emergency sessions, veto override windows) expire automatically and that any network participant may trigger the mechanical enforcement of such expiration without requiring authorization from any governance body.**

**Consciousness Tier: A classification of governance participation eligibility based on a multi-dimensional trust score (identity, reputation, community, engagement). Tiers from lowest to highest: Observer, Participant, Citizen, Steward, Guardian.**

**Emergency Session: A time-limited governance session with elevated decision-making authority, subject to the constraints in Article IX, Section 3. Distinct from technical circuit breakers (Article IX, Section 1) and governance pauses (Article IX, Section 2).**

**Ethics Assessment: A procedural evaluation of a governance proposal's alignment with Core Principles and Member Rights. Produces one of three verdicts: Safe (no concerns), Caution (concerns flagged, disclosed but not escalated), or Blocked (concerns flagged, mandatory disclosure and escalated thresholds required).**

**Participation Insurance: An adaptive mechanism that lowers supermajority override thresholds when quorum is not met, preventing voter suppression from permanently blocking collective action (Article III, Section 6).**

**Guardian Tier: The highest consciousness tier (typically requiring a trust score >= 0.8). Guardian-tier agents may exercise governance powers including the Golden Veto, subject to the constraints in Article III. Instrumental Actors may not attain Guardian tier (Article XI, Section 4).**

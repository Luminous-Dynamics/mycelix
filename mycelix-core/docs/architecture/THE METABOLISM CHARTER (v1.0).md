# **THE METABOLISM CHARTER (v1.1)**

*Updated March 2026: CGC bonus renamed to MYCEL recognition bonus — aligned with production implementation*

**Companion instrument to the Mycelix Spore Constitution (v0.24) and part of the modular Mycelix Charter Set (v2.0).**

---

## Editor's Note: v1.0 (The Living Protocol Extension)

This Charter introduces **temporal coordination** to the Mycelix ecosystem. It provides an optional framework for DAOs to organize their activities around a 28-day metabolism cycle inspired by biological rhythms, regenerative design, and the recognition that different types of work benefit from different temporal contexts.

The Metabolism Charter does not replace any existing governance mechanisms. It adds a temporal layer that DAOs may adopt via MIP-C (Cultural) proposal. DAOs that adopt the Metabolism Cycle gain access to phase-sensitive coordination tools while remaining fully compatible with DAOs that do not.

**Constitutional Alignment**: This Charter operates under the Core Principles of Adaptability and Polycentricity (Constitution Article I, Section 2), enabling communities to choose coordination models aligned with their values.

---

## **PREAMBLE**

Living systems operate in rhythms. Seeds germinate, organisms metabolize, ecosystems cycle through seasons. The Mycelix Network, aspiring to support living coordination, recognizes that human collaboration also benefits from temporal structure—times for letting go, times for reflection, times for creation, and times for commitment.

This Charter provides a framework for temporal coordination that honors these natural rhythms while respecting the autonomy of participating DAOs to adapt the framework to their contexts.

## **ARTICLE I – THE METABOLISM CYCLE**

### **Section 1. Definition and Purpose**

**1.1 The Metabolism Cycle** is a 28-day coordination framework divided into four phases, each providing a distinct context for network activities.

**1.2 Purpose**: The Cycle aims to:

a. Reduce coordination friction by creating shared temporal expectations.

b. Prevent burnout by institutionalizing reflection and release.

c. Improve proposal quality by separating creation from decision-making.

d. Foster regenerative culture by honoring beginnings and endings.

### **Section 2. The Four Phases**

#### **2.1 Release Phase (Days 1-7)**

**Theme**: Letting go of what no longer serves.

**Encouraged Activities**:
- Retrospectives on the previous cycle
- Composting of failed patterns (see Commons Charter extension)
- Wound acknowledgment and healing initiation (see Economic Charter extension)
- Gratitude practices and recognition of contributions
- Archival and documentation of completed work

**Discouraged Activities**:
- Submitting new major proposals (MIP-T, MIP-E, MIP-G)
- Making irreversible commitments
- Starting new large-scope projects

**Phase Ceremony (Optional)**: DAOs may hold a Release Ceremony on Day 1 to collectively acknowledge what is ending.

#### **2.2 Stillness Phase (Days 8-14)**

**Theme**: Holding uncertainty; gathering before deciding.

**Encouraged Activities**:
- Research and exploration
- Stakeholder consultation and input gathering
- Prediction market participation (Epistemic Markets)
- Question formulation (not answering)
- Listening sessions and community dialogue

**Discouraged Activities**:
- Formal voting on binding proposals
- Final decisions on contested matters
- Rushing to resolution

**Phase Ceremony (Optional)**: DAOs may hold a Stillness Ceremony on Day 8 featuring shared silence and question gathering.

#### **2.3 Creation Phase (Days 15-21)**

**Theme**: Forming new patterns; experimentation.

**Encouraged Activities**:
- Drafting and submitting proposals
- Prototyping and experimentation
- Collaborative design sessions
- Sandbox testing of governance innovations
- Creative expression and visioning

**Discouraged Activities**:
- Final ratification votes
- Irreversible resource commitments
- Closing deliberation prematurely

**Phase Ceremony (Optional)**: DAOs may hold a Creation Ceremony on Day 15 to bless experiments and grant permission to fail.

#### **2.4 Integration Phase (Days 22-28)**

**Theme**: Commitment and completion.

**Encouraged Activities**:
- Formal voting on proposals
- Ratification of agreements
- Kenosis offerings (see Economic Charter extension)
- Resource allocation decisions
- Release and deployment of completed work

**Discouraged Activities**:
- Introducing major new proposals
- Reopening settled decisions
- Starting new exploratory work

**Phase Ceremony (Optional)**: DAOs may hold an Integration Ceremony on Day 22 for commitment declarations and witness acknowledgments.

### **Section 3. Cycle Timing**

**3.1 Reference Cycle**: The Global DAO shall maintain a Reference Cycle beginning on the first day of each calendar month containing a new moon. The Knowledge Council shall publish the Reference Cycle calendar annually.

**3.2 Cycle Sovereignty**: Each DAO may operate its own cycle, offset from the Reference Cycle. Common offsets include:
- Aligned (0 days): Synchronized with Reference
- Half-offset (14 days): Complementary rhythm
- Quarter-offset (7 days): Staggered coordination

**3.3 Cycle Registration**: DAOs adopting the Metabolism Cycle shall register their offset with the Knowledge Council's Cycle Registry.

## **ARTICLE II – PHASE-SENSITIVE OPERATIONS**

### **Section 1. Operation Classification**

All governance operations shall be classified according to their phase sensitivity:

| Classification | Definition | Effect |
|----------------|------------|--------|
| **Phase-Encouraged** | Aligned with phase purpose | 1.05x MYCEL recognition bonus for participants |
| **Phase-Neutral** | Neither aligned nor misaligned | Standard operation |
| **Phase-Discouraged** | Contrary to phase purpose | Warning issued; no penalty |
| **Phase-Blocked** | Fundamentally incompatible | Requires governance override |

### **Section 2. Classification Matrix**

| Operation | Release | Stillness | Creation | Integration |
|-----------|---------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Submit MIP | Discouraged | Neutral | Encouraged | Discouraged |
| Vote on MIP | Discouraged | Blocked | Discouraged | Encouraged |
| Create Wound | Encouraged | Neutral | Discouraged | Neutral |
| Advance Wound Phase | Encouraged | Encouraged | Encouraged | Encouraged |
| Commit Kenosis | Neutral | Discouraged | Neutral | Encouraged |
| Start Composting | Encouraged | Neutral | Discouraged | Neutral |
| Submit Prediction | Neutral | Encouraged | Neutral | Neutral |
| Allocate Treasury | Discouraged | Discouraged | Neutral | Encouraged |
| Retrospective | Encouraged | Neutral | Discouraged | Discouraged |
| Research/Exploration | Neutral | Encouraged | Encouraged | Discouraged |

### **Section 3. Override Protocol**

**3.1 Emergency Override**: The Technical Circuit Breaker (Constitution Article IX) may authorize Phase-Blocked operations during declared emergencies.

**3.2 Governance Override**: A DAO may override Phase-Blocked status for a specific operation via:
- Supermajority (⅔) vote of the DAO
- Published justification
- Knowledge Council notification

**3.3 Override Tracking**: All overrides shall be recorded in the Cycle Registry with justification. Repeated overrides (>5 per cycle) trigger Knowledge Council review.

## **ARTICLE III – ADAPTIVE CYCLES**

### **Section 1. Cycle Flexibility**

**1.1 Standard Cycle**: 28 days (4 phases × 7 days each).

**1.2 Compressed Cycle**: For declared emergencies or time-sensitive matters:
- Minimum duration: 7 days (4 phases × ~2 days each)
- Requires: ⅔ supermajority authorization
- Limit: Maximum 2 compressed cycles per calendar year

**1.3 Extended Cycle**: For complex deliberations or deep work:
- Maximum duration: 56 days (4 phases × 14 days each)
- Requires: Simple majority authorization
- Use case: Constitutional amendments, major architecture changes

### **Section 2. Phase Extension**

**2.1 Incomplete Phase**: If a phase's essential work is incomplete, the DAO may extend that phase by up to 7 additional days via simple majority vote.

**2.2 Extension Cascade**: Phase extensions shift subsequent phases. The cycle ends when Integration phase completes, regardless of calendar duration.

**2.3 Extension Limit**: No single cycle may exceed 84 days total (3x standard length).

### **Section 3. Cycle Suspension**

**3.1 Suspension Authority**: A DAO may suspend its Metabolism Cycle during:
- Declared governance emergencies
- Network-wide crises
- Periods of low activity (<10% participation for 2 consecutive cycles)

**3.2 Suspension Process**: Requires simple majority vote with published reasoning.

**3.3 Resumption**: Cycle resumes with a new Release phase upon suspension end.

## **ARTICLE IV – INTER-CYCLE COORDINATION**

### **Section 1. Cross-DAO Synchronization**

**1.1 Sync Points**: DAOs on the Reference Cycle share natural coordination points:
- Release Ceremony (Day 1): Network-wide retrospective
- Integration Close (Day 28): Synchronized commitment window

**1.2 Offset Coordination**: DAOs with different offsets may designate liaison roles to coordinate cross-DAO proposals that span multiple cycle timings.

### **Section 2. Proposal Timing**

**2.1 Cross-DAO Proposals**: Proposals affecting multiple DAOs shall be timed to allow each DAO to process them within their own cycle:
- Submit during any DAO's Creation phase
- Vote during each DAO's Integration phase
- Allow up to 2 cycles for full ratification

**2.2 Urgency Protocol**: Cross-DAO urgent matters may invoke Compressed Cycle for all affected DAOs simultaneously.

### **Section 3. Cycle Metrics**

The Knowledge Council shall track and publish:

a. **Cycle Adoption Rate**: Percentage of DAOs using Metabolism Cycle

b. **Phase Compliance**: Percentage of operations aligned with phase guidance

c. **Override Frequency**: Rate of Phase-Blocked overrides

d. **Ceremony Participation**: Attendance at optional phase ceremonies

e. **Cycle Health Score**: Composite metric informing adoption recommendations

## **ARTICLE V – CULTURAL INTEGRATION**

### **Section 1. Ceremony Templates**

The Knowledge Council shall maintain ceremony templates in the Wisdom Library:

a. **Release Ceremony Template**: Gratitude, acknowledgment, letting go ritual

b. **Stillness Ceremony Template**: Shared silence, question gathering, uncertainty honoring

c. **Creation Ceremony Template**: Blessing of experiments, permission to fail, inspiration sharing

d. **Integration Ceremony Template**: Commitment declarations, witness acknowledgments, cycle closing

### **Section 2. Cultural Naming**

DAOs may adopt cultural names for phases while maintaining interoperability:

| Standard Name | Example Aliases |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Release | Autumn, Exhale, Composting, Descent |
| Stillness | Winter, Pause, Listening, Darkness |
| Creation | Spring, Inhale, Sprouting, Dawn |
| Integration | Summer, Harvest, Fruiting, Zenith |

### **Section 3. Philosophical Alignment**

The Metabolism Cycle draws inspiration from:

a. **Regenerative Design**: Patterns observed in living systems

b. **Negative Capability**: Keats' concept of remaining in uncertainty

c. **Seasonal Wisdom**: Agricultural and indigenous temporal knowledge

d. **Contemplative Practice**: Rhythms of action and reflection

This appendix is non-binding and serves cultural context only.

## **ARTICLE VI – IMPLEMENTATION**

### **Section 1. Adoption Process**

**1.1 DAO Adoption**: A DAO adopts the Metabolism Cycle via:
- MIP-C (Cultural) proposal
- Simple majority vote
- Registration with Knowledge Council Cycle Registry

**1.2 Trial Period**: New adopters enter a 3-cycle (84-day) trial period with:
- Relaxed enforcement (all operations Phase-Neutral)
- Knowledge Council mentorship available
- Trial retrospective at conclusion

**1.3 Full Adoption**: After successful trial, DAO enters full adoption with phase classifications active.

### **Section 2. Technical Infrastructure**

**2.1 Cycle Engine**: The Network shall maintain a Cycle Engine service providing:
- Current phase queries for any registered DAO
- Phase transition webhooks
- Operation classification API
- Cycle calendar exports

**2.2 Integration Points**:
- Governance UI displays current phase and guidance
- Proposal submission warns on Phase-Discouraged timing
- Phase-Blocked operations require explicit override confirmation

### **Section 3. Rollout Timeline**

**Phase 1 (Q2 2026)**: Cycle Engine deployment, Global DAO trial adoption

**Phase 2 (Q3 2026)**: Sector and Regional DAO adoption, ceremony templates released

**Phase 3 (Q4 2026)**: Local DAO adoption, full phase enforcement, metrics dashboard

## **ARTICLE VII – RATIFICATION AND CONTINUITY**

### **Section 1. Charter Ratification**

This Charter takes effect upon:
- Adoption by the Global DAO (simple majority in both houses)
- Ratification by at least 3 Sector DAOs
- Ratification by at least 2 Regional DAOs

### **Section 2. Supremacy Clause**

The Mycelix Spore Constitution (v0.24+) prevails over this Charter in any case of conflict.

### **Section 3. Review Cycle**

This Charter shall be reviewed every 2 years, aligned with the constitutional review cycle. The first review shall assess adoption rates, phase compliance, and cultural reception.

## **APPENDIX A – DEFINITIONS**

**Metabolism Cycle**: A 28-day coordination framework with four phases (Release, Stillness, Creation, Integration).

**Phase**: One of four 7-day periods within a Metabolism Cycle.

**Phase-Encouraged**: Operations aligned with the current phase's purpose.

**Phase-Discouraged**: Operations contrary to the current phase's purpose.

**Phase-Blocked**: Operations fundamentally incompatible with the current phase.

**Reference Cycle**: The Global DAO's Metabolism Cycle, serving as the network-wide reference.

**Cycle Offset**: The number of days a DAO's cycle is shifted from the Reference Cycle.

**Cycle Registry**: A Knowledge Council-maintained registry of DAO cycle adoptions and offsets.

**Phase Ceremony**: An optional ritual marking phase transitions.

**Compressed Cycle**: A shortened cycle (minimum 7 days) for emergency coordination.

**Extended Cycle**: A lengthened cycle (maximum 56 days) for complex deliberation.

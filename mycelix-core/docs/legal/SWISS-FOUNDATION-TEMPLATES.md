> **LEGAL NOTICE**: These templates are provided for informational and planning purposes only. Foundation formation in Switzerland requires engagement with qualified Swiss legal counsel and a notary. Documents must be in an official Swiss language (German, French, or Italian) for formal submission.

---

## Overview

This document contains templates and guidance for establishing a Swiss foundation (Stiftung) to serve as the legal entity for the Mycelix protocol. A Swiss foundation provides:

- Non-profit governance structure
- Regulatory clarity under Swiss law
- FINMA "no-action" letter potential for utility tokens
- Neutral jurisdiction with strong rule of law

---

## 1. Foundation Charter (Stiftungsurkunde)

### 1.1 Template

```
FOUNDATION CHARTER
(Stiftungsurkunde)

of

MYCELIX FOUNDATION
(Mycelix Stiftung)

===

PREAMBLE

The undersigned founder hereby establishes a foundation pursuant to Articles 80 et seq.
of the Swiss Civil Code (ZGB).

---

ARTICLE 1: NAME AND REGISTERED OFFICE

1.1 Under the name "Mycelix Foundation" (Mycelix Stiftung), a foundation within the
    meaning of Articles 80 et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code is established.

1.2 The registered office of the Foundation is in [CANTON], Switzerland.

1.3 The Foundation may establish branch offices and subsidiaries in Switzerland
    and abroad.

---

ARTICLE 2: PURPOSE

2.1 The purpose of the Foundation is:

    a) To develop, maintain, and promote decentralized federated learning technology
       and the Mycelix protocol;

    b) To advance research in privacy-preserving machine learning, cryptography,
       and distributed systems;

    c) To support the Mycelix ecosystem, including developers, validators, and
       participants;

    d) To promote open-source software development for decentralized AI;

    e) To engage in educational activities related to decentralized technology;

    f) To hold, manage, and distribute digital assets in furtherance of the above
       purposes.

2.2 The Foundation pursues non-profit purposes and does not aim at generating
    profit for itself.

2.3 The Foundation may engage in any activities that serve its purpose directly
    or indirectly.

---

ARTICLE 3: ASSETS

3.1 The initial endowment of the Foundation amounts to CHF [AMOUNT] ([AMOUNT IN WORDS]
    Swiss Francs), contributed by the Founder.

3.2 The Foundation may accept donations, bequests, and contributions of any kind
    that serve its purpose.

3.3 The Foundation may hold and manage digital assets, including but not limited to
    cryptocurrencies, tokens, and other blockchain-based assets.

---

ARTICLE 4: FOUNDATION BOARD

4.1 The Foundation Board (Stiftungsrat) is the supreme governing body of the Foundation.

4.2 The Foundation Board shall consist of at least three (3) and not more than
    seven (7) members.

4.3 The initial Foundation Board members are:

    a) [NAME 1], [NATIONALITY], [RESIDENCE]
    b) [NAME 2], [NATIONALITY], [RESIDENCE]
    c) [NAME 3], [NATIONALITY], [RESIDENCE]

4.4 Foundation Board members are appointed for a term of [TWO/THREE] years and may
    be reappointed.

4.5 The Foundation Board appoints its President from among its members.

---

ARTICLE 5: POWERS OF THE FOUNDATION BOARD

5.1 The Foundation Board has the following non-delegable powers:

    a) Ultimate direction of the Foundation and issuance of necessary directives;

    b) Approval of the Foundation's strategy and budget;

    c) Establishment of organizational structure and regulations;

    d) Appointment and removal of persons entrusted with management and representation;

    e) Ultimate supervision of persons entrusted with management;

    f) Approval of annual financial statements and activity reports;

    g) Notification of the supervisory authority of any material changes;

    h) Amendments to the Foundation Charter (subject to supervisory approval);

    i) Dissolution of the Foundation (subject to supervisory approval).

---

ARTICLE 6: REPRESENTATION

6.1 The Foundation is validly represented by the joint signature of two Foundation
    Board members.

6.2 The Foundation Board may grant power of procuration (Prokura) or other
    commercial powers of attorney.

---

ARTICLE 7: AUDITOR

7.1 The Foundation Board shall appoint a qualified auditor licensed by the Swiss
    Federal Audit Oversight Authority (FAOA).

7.2 The auditor shall audit the annual financial statements and report to the
    Foundation Board.

---

ARTICLE 8: SUPERVISION

8.1 The Foundation is subject to the supervision of the [FEDERAL/CANTONAL]
    supervisory authority for foundations.

---

ARTICLE 9: DISSOLUTION AND LIQUIDATION

9.1 The Foundation may be dissolved by resolution of the Foundation Board,
    subject to approval by the supervisory authority.

9.2 Upon dissolution, any remaining assets shall be transferred to another
    tax-exempt organization in Switzerland pursuing similar purposes.

9.3 Distribution of assets to the Founder, Foundation Board members, or their
    related parties is excluded.

---

ARTICLE 10: MISCELLANEOUS

10.1 In all matters not regulated by this Charter, the provisions of Swiss law,
     in particular the Swiss Civil Code, shall apply.

10.2 The authoritative version of this Charter is in [GERMAN/FRENCH/ITALIAN].

---

SIGNATURES

Place and Date: [CITY], [DATE]

The Founder:


_________________________
[FOUNDER NAME]


NOTARIAL CERTIFICATION

[NOTARY CERTIFICATION TEXT]
```

## 2. Foundation Regulations (Stiftungsreglement)

### 2.1 Template

```
FOUNDATION REGULATIONS
(Stiftungsreglement)

of

MYCELIX FOUNDATION

===

Based on Article 5 of the Foundation Charter, the Foundation Board hereby adopts
the following Foundation Regulations:

---

SECTION I: FOUNDATION BOARD

Article 1: Composition and Appointment

1.1 The Foundation Board consists of [3-7] members.

1.2 New members are elected by the existing Foundation Board with a two-thirds
    majority.

1.3 At least one member must be resident in Switzerland.

1.4 Foundation Board members serve for a term of [2/3] years and may be reappointed.

Article 2: Meetings

2.1 The Foundation Board shall meet at least [FOUR] times per year.

2.2 Meetings may be held in person, by video conference, or by telephone.

2.3 The President convenes meetings with at least [14] days notice.

2.4 Extraordinary meetings may be convened by any two members.

Article 3: Quorum and Voting

3.1 The Foundation Board has a quorum if a majority of members are present.

3.2 Resolutions are passed by simple majority of votes cast.

3.3 The President has the casting vote in case of a tie.

3.4 Written circular resolutions are permitted if no member objects.

Article 4: Compensation

4.1 Foundation Board members may receive reasonable compensation for their services.

4.2 Compensation levels are determined annually and disclosed in the annual report.

---

SECTION II: EXECUTIVE MANAGEMENT

Article 5: Managing Director

5.1 The Foundation Board may appoint a Managing Director (Geschäftsführer).

5.2 The Managing Director is responsible for day-to-day management of the Foundation.

5.3 The Managing Director reports to the Foundation Board.

Article 6: Committees

6.1 The Foundation Board may establish committees for specific purposes:

    a) Technical Committee: Protocol development decisions
    b) Grants Committee: Funding allocation
    c) Governance Committee: Token holder relations

---

SECTION III: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

Article 7: Budget and Reporting

7.1 The Managing Director prepares an annual budget for Foundation Board approval.

7.2 Quarterly financial reports are presented to the Foundation Board.

7.3 Annual financial statements are prepared in accordance with Swiss GAAP FER.

Article 8: Investment Policy

8.1 Foundation assets shall be managed prudently to preserve capital and generate
    returns consistent with the Foundation's risk tolerance.

8.2 Digital asset holdings are subject to the following guidelines:

    a) Maximum [X]% in any single digital asset (excluding [TOKEN NAME])
    b) Custody with qualified custodians
    c) Multi-signature controls for large transactions

Article 9: Reserves

9.1 The Foundation shall maintain operating reserves equivalent to at least
    [12/24] months of operating expenses.

---

SECTION IV: GRANTS AND FUNDING

Article 10: Grant Programs

10.1 The Foundation may operate grant programs to support ecosystem development.

10.2 Grant criteria and procedures are established by the Grants Committee.

10.3 All grants above CHF [AMOUNT] require Foundation Board approval.

Article 11: Conflict of Interest

11.1 Foundation Board members and staff must disclose conflicts of interest.

11.2 Conflicted parties abstain from related decisions.

---

SECTION V: TOKEN GOVERNANCE

Article 12: Token Holder Rights

12.1 [TOKEN NAME] holders may participate in governance through on-chain voting.

12.2 The Foundation commits to implementing proposals that:

    a) Achieve the required voting threshold
    b) Are technically feasible
    c) Are legally permissible
    d) Are consistent with the Foundation's purpose

Article 13: Governance Proposals

13.1 Types of proposals:

    a) Protocol parameters: [X]% threshold
    b) Grant allocations: [X]% threshold
    c) Charter amendments: [X]% threshold (supermajority)

---

SECTION VI: FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 14: Amendments

14.1 These Regulations may be amended by the Foundation Board with a two-thirds
     majority.

Article 15: Entry into Force

15.1 These Regulations enter into force upon adoption by the Foundation Board.

---

Adopted by the Foundation Board on [DATE].

[SIGNATURES]
```

## 3. Board Composition Proposal

### 3.1 Recommended Structure

| Position | Qualifications | Initial Term |
|----------|----------------|--------------|
| President | Swiss resident, governance experience | 3 years |
| Technical Lead | Protocol/cryptography expertise | 2 years |
| Legal/Compliance | Swiss regulatory experience | 2 years |
| Community Representative | Ecosystem engagement | 2 years |
| Independent Member | Cross-industry perspective | 2 years |

### 3.2 Selection Criteria

**Essential Qualifications**:
- No conflicts of interest with competing projects
- Background check clearance
- Commitment to Foundation purpose
- Availability for quarterly meetings

**Desirable Qualifications**:
- Experience with blockchain/crypto governance
- International network and reputation
- Fluency in English and a Swiss language

### 3.3 Diversity Considerations

- Geographic: At least 1 Swiss, 1 EU, 1 non-EU member
- Gender: Target minimum 30% underrepresented genders
- Background: Technical, legal, business mix

## 5. Formation Checklist

### 5.1 Pre-Formation

- [ ] Engage Swiss legal counsel
- [ ] Engage Swiss notary
- [ ] Draft Foundation Charter in official language
- [ ] Draft Foundation Regulations
- [ ] Identify initial Foundation Board members
- [ ] Conduct background checks on board members
- [ ] Prepare initial endowment (minimum CHF 50,000 recommended)
- [ ] Select registered office address in Switzerland
- [ ] Identify supervisory authority (federal or cantonal)

### 5.2 Formation Process

- [ ] Execute Foundation Charter before notary
- [ ] Register with Commercial Registry
- [ ] Obtain tax exemption status (if applicable)
- [ ] Open Swiss bank account
- [ ] Notify supervisory authority
- [ ] Appoint auditor
- [ ] Establish digital asset custody

### 5.3 Post-Formation

- [ ] Submit FINMA classification request
- [ ] Establish governance procedures
- [ ] Implement AML/KYC procedures
- [ ] Set up accounting and reporting
- [ ] Publish transparency disclosures

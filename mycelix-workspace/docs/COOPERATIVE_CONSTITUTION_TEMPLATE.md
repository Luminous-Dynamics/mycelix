# Mycelix Cooperative Constitution Template

**Legal Framework:** Co-operatives Act, 2005 (Act No. 14 of 2005), Republic of South Africa

**Purpose:** Template for communities deploying Mycelix as a registered cooperative. Adapt names, addresses, and governance thresholds to your community.

---

## Article 1: Name and Registration

1.1. The cooperative shall be known as **[Community Name] Resilience Cooperative** (hereinafter "the Cooperative").

1.2. The Cooperative shall be registered as a **primary cooperative** under the Co-operatives Act, 2005.

1.3. The registered address shall be [address].

## Article 2: Purpose and Scope

2.1. The Cooperative exists to facilitate **mutual aid and time exchange** among its members using the Mycelix platform.

2.2. The Cooperative operates a **TEND time exchange system** wherein members exchange hours of service. One TEND credit represents one hour of community labor.

2.3. TEND credits are **internal accounting units** of the Cooperative. They are:
- Not legal tender
- Not redeemable for South African Rand or any other currency
- Not transferable to non-members
- Valid only within the Cooperative

2.4. The Cooperative may operate a **community price discovery system** (Value Anchor) to track the local purchasing power of labor in terms of real goods and services.

2.5. The Cooperative may operate **food production tracking, mutual aid coordination, and emergency communication systems** for community resilience.

## Article 3: Membership

3.1. Membership is open to any natural person residing within the Cooperative's area of operation who:
- (a) Agrees to this Constitution
- (b) Is at least 18 years of age
- (c) Completes the identity verification process

3.2. Each member holds an **equal share** (1/N) in the Cooperative. Shares are not tradeable.

3.3. Members participate in governance according to the **consciousness tier system**:

| Tier | Requirements | Rights |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Observer | Identity verified | View community data |
| Citizen | Identity (≥0.25) + Reputation (≥0.10) | Exchange TEND, report prices, vote |
| Steward | Sustained participation + community recognition | Propose governance motions |
| Guardian | Demonstrated community leadership | Audit systems, lead committees |
| Sage | Long-term commitment + consensus of Guardians | Amend constitution |

3.4. Tier advancement is determined by demonstrated community participation, not by financial contribution.

3.5. A member may voluntarily withdraw by notifying the Cooperative in writing. Upon withdrawal:
- Outstanding TEND credits owed to the member are forgiven
- Outstanding TEND debts owed by the member are forgiven
- The member's data is retained for audit purposes for 5 years

## Article 4: Governance

4.1. The Cooperative is governed by its members on the principle of **one member, one vote**.

4.2. Governance decisions require:
- **Ordinary resolution:** Simple majority of votes cast
- **Special resolution:** 75% of votes cast (for constitutional amendments, dissolution)
- **Emergency resolution:** Triggered by volatility escalation in the Value Anchor system

4.3. Voting weight for economic governance may be adjusted by the **accuracy-weighted oracle system**, which reflects demonstrated reliability in price reporting, not wealth or status.

4.4. The Cooperative shall maintain a **board of directors** of not fewer than 3 and not more than 9 members, elected annually by ordinary resolution.

## Article 5: Financial Affairs

5.1. The Cooperative's TEND exchange system operates on a **zero-sum mutual credit** basis. The aggregate of all member balances is always zero.

5.2. TEND credit limits are set by governance:
- Standard: ±40 TEND
- New members (first 90 days): ±10 TEND
- Emergency (as determined by the Value Anchor oracle): up to ±120 TEND

5.3. The Cooperative does not hold, manage, or transmit South African Rand or any other legal tender on behalf of members.

5.4. Members acknowledge that **barter transactions may have tax implications** under the Income Tax Act. The Cooperative provides a **tax export tool** that generates records of TEND exchanges valued at the South African National Minimum Wage rate, suitable for SARS reporting.

5.5. The Cooperative is not a bank, money transmitter, or crypto asset service provider. It does not provide financial services as defined in the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act (FAIS) or the Financial Sector Regulation Act (FSRA).

## Article 6: Data and Privacy

6.1. Member data is stored on members' own devices using the Holochain agent-centric architecture. The Cooperative does not operate a central database.

6.2. Each member's **source chain** (transaction history) is controlled by that member.

6.3. The Cooperative may operate a **mesh network** for offline synchronization. Data transmitted over the mesh is encrypted and authenticated.

6.4. Members consent to their TEND exchange records being visible to their direct counterparties and to the consensus computation system.

## Article 7: Price Oracle and Value Anchor

7.1. The Cooperative operates a **community price discovery system** (Value Anchor) that tracks local prices of essential goods in TEND units.

7.2. Price reports are voluntary and weighted by **historical accuracy** (EMA), not by member status or financial contribution.

7.3. The **Roodepoort Resilience Basket** (or equivalent community basket) provides a composite index of local purchasing power. Basket composition is approved by governance.

7.4. If the basket index shows weekly volatility exceeding 20%, the system may automatically adjust TEND credit limits to protect community stability.

## Article 8: Dispute Resolution

8.1. Disputes between members regarding TEND exchanges follow a three-stage process:
- **Stage 1:** Direct negotiation between parties (7 days)
- **Stage 2:** Mediation by a panel of 3 Steward-tier members (14 days)
- **Stage 3:** Governance vote by all Citizen-tier members (binding)

8.2. Disputes regarding governance decisions may be referred to the **Co-operatives Tribunal** established under the Co-operatives Act.

## Article 9: Amendment and Dissolution

9.1. This Constitution may be amended by **special resolution** (75% of votes cast) at a general meeting with at least 14 days' notice.

9.2. Upon dissolution, all TEND balances are zeroed. Any remaining assets of the Cooperative shall be distributed equally among members or donated to a registered non-profit organization serving the community, as determined by special resolution.
